Hello World: Restarting My Blog

Hello World.

This is the first updated note of my personal blog. I want this site to become a small but steady place for recording research ideas, paper reading notes, technical experiments, and reflections from daily learning.

The original Hello World post was created by Hexo as a default example. Today I am turning it into a real starting point. From now on, this blog will be managed in the Hexo source folder, generated into static HTML, and deployed to GitHub Pages.

Why keep a blog

A research blog is useful because it gives unfinished thoughts a stable place to grow. Not every idea needs to become a paper immediately. Some ideas first need a page, a date, a few references, and a clearer question.

For me, this blog will mainly be used for three things:

  1. recording new ideas before they disappear;
  2. summarizing useful papers and technical tools;
  3. connecting scattered notes into longer-term research directions.

Current plan

The next posts will focus on AI, biomedical data, drug discovery, and practical research workflows. I also want to keep notes about code, visualization, and reproducible experiments.

This post is small, but it marks the moment when the website starts becoming a real notebook.
