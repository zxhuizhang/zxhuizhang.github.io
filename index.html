Assistant Researcher, Hangzhou Institute of Medicine, CAS / Ph.D., The University of Tokyo

Zhang Xin

AI-assisted drug discovery, peptide design, natural product screening, molecular dynamics, and drug repositioning

Zhang Xin is an Assistant Researcher at the Hangzhou Institute of Medicine, Chinese Academy of Sciences, and received his Ph.D. from The University of Tokyo. His research covers AI-assisted small-molecule and peptide drug design, natural product screening based on protein and ligand structures, molecular dynamics simulation, and drug repositioning algorithms.

  • Research interests span AI4S, AIDD, MD simulation, protein-ligand interaction, and peptide drug design.
  • Member of CCF, the CCF Bioinformatics Technical Committee, and CBI Society.
  • Reviewer and young editorial board member for academic journals and conferences, with a focus on interpretable AI and structure-based drug discovery.

Research Areas

Research Areas

AI-assisted molecular and peptide drug design

Integrating generative models, sequence/structure representations, and pharmacological constraints for interpretable candidate design.

Natural product and structure-based virtual screening

Using natural product databases, protein structures, and ligand structures for lead discovery against targets such as Mpro and VP37.

Drug-target interaction and repositioning algorithms

Developing multimodal, multi-structural, and debiased learning frameworks for robust DTI/DPI prediction.

Molecular dynamics and mechanism interpretation

Strengthening AI predictions with molecular dynamics, binding stability analysis, and structural explanation.
